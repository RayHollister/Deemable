Episode 10 – The Two Step Verification, Baby

by Ray Hollister • 08/13/2012 • 1 Comment

This week Ray Hollister, Tom Braun, Kevin Clark, and Aaron Edelson we introduced a new segment of the show that we’re calling “The Deemable Dozen”, the top twelve tech news stories in under 12 minutes, and then talked about how Gizmodo reporter Mat Honan’s digital life was destroyed, and then we did the Two Step Verification, baby. (You’ll have to listen to understand.)

…all that and more on this episode of Deemable Tech.

Deemable Tech – Episode 10 – The Two Step Verification, Baby

The Deemable Dozen:

  1. Sprint reduces iPhone 4S to $149.99 and Apple prices matches.
  2. 3 guys try to buy cheap iPads off Craigslist and get robbed instead.
  3. Google Nexus Q production postponed, but you’ll get it for if you already placed a pre-order.
  4. Before Apple went nuclear on Samsung, Apple offered Samsung a licensing agreement: $30 for every smartphone and $40 for every tablet.
  5. Google Listen is dead! Long live all the other podcast apps in Google Play!
  6. Microsoft asks everyone to stop calling it Metro; call it Windows 8.
  7. Square and Starbucks partnered up,  and ordered Orange Mocha Frappuccinos for everyone!
  8. Everyone else in the mobile payments industry except for Square or Starbucks (including AT&T, Sprint, T-Mobile, Verizon, Google, Intuit, ISIS, PayPal and several banks) joined the Mobile Payments Committee of the Electronics Transactions Association.
  9. OUYA ended its Kickstarter campaign 8.65 million over their goal! Now OUYA has started accepting preorders.
  10. Apollo 13 was really saved by a hippy at MIT?
  11. WikiLeaks is under attack.
  12. Mat Honan got hacked.
Today’s Questions:
  1. Did my email change when Xfinity bought out Comcast ?
  2. My Wi-Fi doesn’t reach my entire house? How can I make my Wi-Fi network bigger?
  3. Why aren’t my hashtags showing up in Twitter search?
