RAY: There’s a few options: eBay, Craigslist, Etsy, ArtFire and a bunch of other Etsy knock offs.

Craigslist- Free, but expectation is to only do business locally so you have to meet people in person.

Ebay, don’t pay until you buy, auction style often drives prices low because it’s a buyers market.

Etsy. Go where the shoppers are.

Remember you can get a URL and direct it to your Etsy page, so you could get SerenityNook.com and direct it to your Etsy Page.

Dean asked: Where’s a good place to get a replacement battery for my Dell laptop? It’s Model number PP31L.

RAY: OK, so that’s a Dell Studio 1735/1737.

Generally, I would start with the manufacturer to get a baseline price, and then I would check out a couple of other sites for comparison.

http://www.amazon.com/

http://www.newegg.com/

http://www.tigerdirect.com/

http://www.batteriesplus.com/

I looked on Dell’s website, and they had two different batteries for that model, one was about $110, the other was about $150. Obviously, the more expensive one was the better one that lasts longer.

Online I found them going for anywhere between $40 -120.

If you just want to find the cheapest replacement battery that you can fine, go to Google and just type in Dell studio 1735 battery and look at the shopping results. That will give you a breakdown of pretty much every battery that’s out there for the Dell studio 1735 or 1737. And then you can sort it by cheapest to most expensive or most relevant or any way you want to.

What you want to keep in mind though is quality and customer service. If you buy a battery from some website in some foreign country that has no customer service number, but you get a cheap deal on the battery, you can expect that you’re probably not going to get any service if the battery breaks down a month after you buy it. Sometimes it’s worth buying something that’s more expensive, but you know it’s going to last, or if it doesn’t you know the company that sold to you is going to replace it for you.

Jennifer wrote in: I listened to your segment this morning on WJCT about protecting the iPad & iPhone. I have the Blackberry Bold 9900 & Blackberry Playbook 64gb tablet. I would like a recommendation for apps to protect, lock down & secure the Blackberry Bold & Blackberry Playbook tablet. Thanks for all your help!

RAY: Well first of all thanks for email Jennifer. Second of all, sorry it took so long to get back to you. Looks like it’s in this email back in April, and it looks like We must’ve missed placed your email. So, sorry about that!

I had to go back and find out which radio segment you were talking about, and I found out it was the one about finding a lost iPhone.

Unfortunately BlackBerry did not have a built-in feature like Find my iPhone until BlackBerry 10. BlackBerry does make an app though that you can download for the BlackBerry Bold 9900 called BlackBerry Protect. It’s free, and it lets you do basically everything that Find my iPhone does. It will let you locate it, lock it, make it ring loud, put a message on the screen, and wipe it if you need to. You can even back up your data remotely before you wipe it.

Now, for your BlackBerry playbook, Oh! You’re the one who bought a BlackBerry playbook. I was wondering who that was.

The BlackBerry Playbook is an odd duck because it is the only device that runs the operating system it runs. And when blackberry puts out new tablets (whenever that is) they probably are not going to run that version of the operating system either. They’re probably going to run BlackBerry 10.

And that is why BlackBerry Protect does not work on the BlackBerry Playbook, even though it is built into BlackBerry 10.
